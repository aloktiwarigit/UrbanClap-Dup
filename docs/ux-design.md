## 2. Core Experience

The single most important UX truth about homeservices-mvp:

> **"The product feels like it respects everyone's time."**

Riya books in 90 seconds. Suresh accepts a job in 30 seconds with full context. Alokt runs operations solo in 22 minutes a day. Priya stays safe in moments that would be terrifying elsewhere. The whole system earns its keep because every screen gets out of the way of what the user came to do — while doing beautifully the trust-building a home-services marketplace demands.

### 2.1 Three Signature Moments

These are the "I get it" moments. If these land, everything else can be tweaked.

**Signature Moment 1 — Customer's First Booking.** Riya taps the app. OTP via Truecaller (no typing). Photo-first catalogue loads. She taps "AC Deep Service" — the service-detail screen hits her with *"₹599. Here's what you get. Here's what could change the price. Here's Suresh who would come: 4.8★, ITI-certified, DigiLocker verified, 5 jobs done in your society."* She books. **Time: 48 seconds.**

**Signature Moment 2 — Technician's Offer Card.** Suresh's phone buzzes. He sees: *"AC deep service in DLF Phase 3, Sunday 8 AM. You earn ₹450 of ₹599. 4.3 km travel, ~45 min. You got this because you're 3rd-nearest with 4.8★ rating on AC service."* He hits Accept. **That transparent earnings preview + algorithm honesty is the vendor loyalty moment.**

**Signature Moment 3 — Owner's Live Map.** Alokt opens the admin at 7 AM. Dashboard greets him in < 3 seconds. City map with 12 colored pins showing every tech. Real-time order feed ticks as a new booking comes in and Suresh's pin turns orange (en route). **One screen = entire business operations. No ops team needed.**

### 5.3 Spacing — 4pt Grid

| Token | px | Usage |

| `space.2` | 8 | Tight spacing (label → input) |

### 5.4 Motion Tokens

| Token | Duration | Easing | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| `motion.fast` | 150 ms | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` | Micro-interactions (button press, icon transform) |
| `motion.base` | 200 ms | Spring 0.8 / 0.4 | Default transitions (cards in, tabs switch) |
| `motion.medium` | 300 ms | Spring 0.7 / 0.35 | Modal enter, bottom-sheet reveal |
| `motion.slow` | 500 ms | `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` | Onboarding illustrations, celebration moments |

**Rule:** every state change has 200ms spring feedback. Never gratuitous (Brainstorm §12.2 principle #7).

### 6.4 Voice & Tone

**Writing style** (applied across all 3 surfaces):

- **Concise** — one sentence where two would do
- **Direct but warm** — "Book for Sunday 8 AM" not "Please select your preferred booking date"
- **Never condescending** — users are smart; tech jargon when accurate
- **Hindi copywriting-aware** — direct Hindi translations of English tech-marketing-speak often feel weird; write natively in both
- **Confidence, not hype** — "Your tech arrived on time 95% of the time" not "🎉 Super-reliable service!"

Example strings:

| Screen | Copy |
|---|---|
| OTP screen title | "Enter the code we sent to ****1234" |
| Tech matching | "Finding your technician…" |
| Tech assigned | "Suresh is on the way. ETA 12 min." |
| Price lock info | "This price is locked. Any add-ons need your approval." |
| Rating shield | "Want the owner to fix this first? They'll respond in 2 hours." |
| Tech no-show | "Sorry about that. We've added ₹500 credit and found someone else." |
| Owner live ops | "All systems normal. 47 bookings today, 12 techs on duty." |

## 7. Design Directions (Three Surfaces Detailed)

### 7.1 Customer Android App — Design Direction

**Mood:** Warm hospitality + trust signals. Airbnb-crossed-with-Cult.fit.

**Key screens:**

1. **Onboarding (3 screens):** Illustration hero → "Book home services, done right" → OTP/Truecaller/Google auth → Permission ask (FCM) → Home.
2. **Home:** Photo-first catalogue. Curated "Services popular this week in your area" (P2, C-14 deferred). Single bold CTA: no hero banner if not needed. Categories as large photo cards, not tiny icons.
3. **Service Detail:** Large hero photo (full-bleed). Price in `title.lg` 28px. Below: "Includes" (checklist), "Possible add-ons" (table with trigger conditions), FAQ accordion, reviews.
4. **Booking flow:** 3 screens max — Slot → Address (Google Maps pin + saved addresses) → Confirm & pay. Final screen shows Trust Dossier of assigned tech (tech is dispatched on-demand post-payment).
5. **Tracking screen (post-payment):** Live map with tech's position + ETA pill at top. Tech card (photo, name, Trust Dossier summary) at bottom. Status timeline below: Searching → Assigned → En route → Reached → Working → Done.
6. **Service in progress:** Large status with photo updates from tech. Subtle Safety icon top-right. Chat button.
7. **Service complete:** Before/after photos side-by-side. Price confirmation. Single rating prompt (stars + 1 field for comment + optional tip prompt).
8. **Profile / History:** Cards of past services. Re-book button on each. Trusted techs list.

**Design principle per screen:** one hero CTA, generous whitespace (2x UC), photo presence, single accent color usage.

### 7.2 Technician Android App — Design Direction

**Mood:** CRED + Linear. Information-dense, fast, respectful.

**Key screens:**

1. **Home (Earnings-first):** Today's earnings counter (large, `display.lg`) + jobs count + monthly progress bar. Job offers inbox below. Quick actions: Go online/offline toggle.
2. **Job Offer Card:** Full-screen takeover when offer arrives. Shows: Earnings preview prominent, service + customer details, travel + duration, "Why you got this" transparency line. Big Accept / Decline buttons. 30-sec timer visible.
3. **Active Job Workflow:** Single screen with big status button that transforms (Started Trip → Reached → Start Work → Add Parts → Complete). Camera prompts auto-open at photo stages.
4. **Parts Catalog:** (Phase 2, T-8) barcode scan + search + add to bill. Customer approves on their phone.
5. **Earnings Dashboard:** Today / Week / Month tabs. Goal progress bar if goal set. Per-job breakdown. Payout cadence selector (Weekly free / Next-day ₹15 / Instant ₹25).
6. **Profile:** Public-facing portfolio builder. Completed jobs count, rating breakdown, languages, specialties, certifications, kit photos.
7. **Community Board** (Phase 2, T-20): Feed of questions + karma system.
8. **Appeals screen:** List of past ratings with <3★ appeal buttons. Evidence upload flow.
9. **Settings + wallet:** Tech welfare toggles (T-17/T-18/T-19 Phase 2).

**Design principle:** utility + respect. Dense but readable. Actions big and obvious. Earnings prominence everywhere.

### 7.3 Owner Web Admin — Design Direction

**Mood:** Stripe Dashboard + Linear + Figma FigJam. Data-rich, fast, keyboard-friendly.

**Key screens:**

1. **Live Ops Dashboard (default landing):** Left panel: city map with live tech pins (colored by status). Right panel: real-time order feed (last 20 events, auto-scrolling). Top bar: today's counters (Bookings, GMV, Commission, Payouts pending, Complaints).
2. **Orders Module:** Data table (Linear-style) with filters, sorting, column config. Row click → drill-down slide-over (Stripe-style) with full order context.
3. **Order Detail (slide-over):** Timeline + Customer + Tech + Photos + Payment + Refunds + Complaints + Internal Notes. Override actions (Re-assign, Manual Complete, Refund, Escalate) in a sticky footer with reason-code required.
4. **Tech Roster:** Sortable table — Rating, This-week earnings, Lifetime GMV, Status, Last-active, Location. Per-row actions: Activate/Deactivate (with reason), Commission tune (Phase 2), KYC refresh, Chat (Phase 2).
5. **Finance Module:** Daily P&L line chart top. Below: tabbed tables for Payouts Pending / Recent Disbursements / Refunds / GST Register / Gateway Reconciliation. Weekly Payout Queue UI with "Approve All" + per-tech approve/hold.
6. **Complaints Inbox:** Kanban-style columns (New / Acknowledged / Resolved / Reopened) with SLA timer on each card. Drag to change status.
7. **Audit Log:** Searchable log viewer with filters (user, date, action-type). Immutable — no edit/delete actions available.
8. **Catalogue Module (Phase 2):** Category / Service CRUD with inline editing. Publish / Unpublish toggle. Pricing simulator.
9. **Marketing Module (Phase 2):** Coupons CRUD + Referral config + Push-campaign composer (target by topic).
10. **Regulatory Dashboard (Phase 2, O-30):** SSC levy calculated + quarterly remittance status, Karnataka welfare board contributions, GST filings.

**Design principle:** every screen fast (< 3s load), every action < 2 clicks from Live Ops landing, every override traceable.

## 10. UX Patterns

### 10.1 Loading States

- **Every wait is a skeleton**, never a generic spinner
- Skeleton shimmer in `neutral.100` → `neutral.50` oscillation, 1.5 s cycle
- Only exception: OTP sending (2 s max) and dispatcher search (30 s max) — use animated illustration instead

### 10.2 Empty States

- Custom illustration + warm one-line copy + clear next action CTA
- Examples:
  - No bookings yet: "Your first home service, one tap away" + Browse button
  - No techs in area: "We're just opening up here. Want to be notified when we're ready?" + Notify button
  - No complaints: "All quiet on the complaint front. Nice work." — inspirational but honest

### 10.3 Error States

- Never blame the user
- Always offer recovery action
- Format: *"[What went wrong]. [What user can do]."*
- Example: "Couldn't connect to our servers. Check your network and try again?" + Retry button

### 10.4 Notification & Alert Patterns

- Low-priority: Toast (auto-dismiss 4 s)
- Medium: Banner (stays until dismissed)
- High: Modal (interrupts flow)
- Critical: Full-screen takeover (SOS, force majeure)

### 10.5 FCM Notification Design

Rich notifications with:
- Title (action-oriented): "Suresh is on the way"
- Body (context): "ETA 12 min — DLF Phase 3"
- Hero image (optional): tech photo or status graphic
- Action buttons: "Track" or "Message tech"

### 10.6 Dark Mode

- **First-class, not afterthought** (NFR-A-3)
- Every screen designed in both themes from day 1
- Paparazzi screenshot tests cover both
- Default follows system; user override in settings

### 10.7 Microinteractions

- 200 ms spring feedback on every interaction (button press, card tap, toggle)
- Haptic feedback (subtle) on booking success, payment success, rating submit
- Success celebration on first booking + 50th job milestone for tech (confetti at minimum, Lottie animation)

### 10.8 Progressive Disclosure

- Never show 30 options at once (UC's mistake)
- "Show more" accordions on Service Detail FAQ
- "See all reviews" expands to infinite-scroll
- Settings grouped logically; advanced settings behind 1 click

### 10.9 Form Design

- One question per screen for critical forms (onboarding, booking)
- Sections grouped semantically (Address vs Slot vs Payment)
- Inline validation on blur, never on each keystroke
- Errors in `semantic.danger` text below the field with icon

## 11. Responsive & Accessibility

### 11.1 Responsive Strategy

**Admin web (Next.js):**
- Desktop-first (owner primarily uses laptop/desktop)
- Breakpoints: `sm 640px`, `md 768px`, `lg 1024px`, `xl 1280px`, `2xl 1536px`
- Data tables collapse to stacked cards at `sm`
- Sidebar becomes bottom drawer at `md`
- Fully functional at tablet width (1024 px); graceful on mobile (usable but not optimized)

**Mobile apps:**
- Portrait-only (landscape not supported)
- Screen sizes supported: 360×640 min, 412×892 typical, 428×926 max
- Safe-area aware (notch, dynamic island)
- Support foldables (basic — display scales to larger folded state)

### 11.2 Accessibility Compliance

Per NFR-A (1–5):

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** target across all surfaces
- Every color combination verified via axe-core CI gate
- Keyboard navigation: all admin functions accessible via keyboard (NFR-A-1)
- TalkBack / VoiceOver tested on critical flows (NFR-A-2)
- Dark-mode parity (NFR-A-3)
- Minimum 14px text (NFR-A-4), 4.5:1 contrast (NFR-A-5)
- Large-font-mode (Phase 2, 1.2× scale)

### 11.3 Multilingual Typography

- Hindi: Noto Sans Devanagari (free, Google Fonts)
- Tamil: Noto Sans Tamil
- Bengali: Noto Sans Bengali
- Max-string-length tests in Figma for layout integrity (Hindi often 1.3x English width)
- Right-to-left: not required (all MVP+Phase 2 languages are LTR)

### 11.4 Motion Sensitivity

- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` OS setting
- Reduced-motion alternative: fade-only transitions, no spring animation
- Haptic feedback opt-out-able in settings
